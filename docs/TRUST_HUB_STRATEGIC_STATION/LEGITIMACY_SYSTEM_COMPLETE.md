### What It Covers

#### 1. **Participation Criteria** (Who Can Join)
- Identity verification (KYC) — Not optional
- Financial verification — Must have real bank account
- Behavioral commitment — Must accept arbitration process
- Entry restrictions — Who is automatically rejected (fraudsters, sanctioned persons, illegal businesses)
- Entry levels — Provisional → Basic → Active progression

**Key insight:** Entry is not free. You must prove you are who you claim to be.

---

#### 2. **Verifiable vs Declared Reputation**
- **Declared:** "I have 10 years of experience" (meaningless)
- **Validated:** "47 projects completed, 92% on time, 4.6/5 rating, zero disputes" (meaningful)

**Three dimensions of verifiable reputation:**

**A) Completion** 
- Do you finish what you start?
- Measured: Project completion rate, on-time delivery %, timeline accuracy

**B) Quality**
- Do deliverables meet agreement?
- Measured: Customer satisfaction (1–5 scale), dispute rate, revision requests

**C) Accountability**
- Do you own your mistakes?
- Measured: Dispute resolution patterns, how you handle criticism, refund acceptance

---

#### 3. **Trust Score (Aggregate Signal)**
- Completion (40% weight) + Quality (35% weight) + Accountability (25% weight) = 0–100 score
- Visible to public; influences who gets which opportunities
- Decays over time without continued good behavior

**Key insight:** Your reputation is your currency. It's earned, visible, and consequential.

---

#### 4. **Conflict Categories (Not All Disputes Are the Same)**

**Type 1: Transactional** (<€5K, straightforward)
- Example: "Is this deliverable complete?"
- Who decides: Automated arbiter
- Timeline: 72 hours

**Type 2: Behavioral** (Pattern across multiple projects)
- Example: "Expert has 4 disputes in 6 months"
- Who decides: Governance committee
- Timeline: 1–2 weeks

**Type 3: Systemic** (Affects multiple participants or sets precedent)
- Example: "Discovered multi-account fraud pattern"
- Who decides: Board + external legal
- Timeline: 2–4 weeks

**Type 4: Criminal** (Fraud, theft, harassment)
- Example: "Expert claims company stole their code"
- Who decides: Law enforcement
- Timeline: Immediate referral

---

#### 5. **Mediation vs Arbitration**

**Mediation** (Self-resolution)
- TRUST HUB facilitates, doesn't decide
- 7-day window
- Success rate: ~40%

**Arbitration** (Binding decision)
- Arbiter decides based on contract + evidence
- 72 hours (urgent) or 7 days (standard)
- Arbiter doesn't know participant status/value

**Appeal** (Governance review)
- Reviews for process errors, not evidence re-examination
- 14 days
- ~10% of appeals overturn

---

#### 6. **Where TRUST HUB Authority Ends**

TRUST HUB does NOT:
- ❌ Set prices (market does)
- ❌ Control scope (parties do)
- ❌ Define timeline (parties do)
- ❌ Define quality (parties do)
- ❌ Provide refunds outside arbitration
- ❌ Enforce work methods
- ❌ Engage in offline disputes
- ❌ Handle criminal conduct (law enforcement does)

**Key insight:** Infrastructure sets process, not outcomes. This is the limit of legitimate authority.

---

#### 7. **Abuse Prevention Mechanisms**

**How TRUST HUB prevents corruption:**

1. **Anonymized arbitration** — Arbiters don't know participant value or status
2. **Randomized assignment** — Cannot build relationships with "friendly" arbiters
3. **Automatic recusal** — Conflicts of interest automatically remove arbiters
4. **Published decisions** — All decisions visible; patterns reveal bias
5. **External audit** — Independent auditor checks annually for corruption
6. **Founder recusal** — Founder doesn't arbitrate disputes

**Key insight:** Corruption is impossible when processes are structural, not personal.

## 🔑 Key Insights From Legitimacy Document

### 1. Reputation is Structural, Not Subjective
You cannot claim "we value reputation." Reputation must be:
- Measurable (not declared)
- Transparent (visible to all)
- Consequential (affects opportunities)
- Decay-able (must be maintained)

### 2. Trust is Earned, Not Promised
TRUST HUB doesn't ask participants to "trust us." It asks them to:
- **Verify KYC** (we know who you are)
- **Validate reputation** (see what we can prove)
- **Observe arbitration** (decisions are explained)
- **Audit processes** (external auditors check us)

### 3. Fairness Must Be Structural
You cannot trust "the founder will be fair." Fairness must be:
- Anonymized (arbiter doesn't know power imbalance)
- Randomized (cannot build corrupt relationships)
- Published (decisions are visible)
- Audited (external eyes check for bias)

### 4. Limits of Authority Matter
TRUST HUB has clear boundaries. It does NOT:
- Set prices, control timelines, define quality
- Refund outside arbitration, enforce methods
- Handle crimes, mediate offline disputes

This is important: **Infrastructure respects autonomy.**
