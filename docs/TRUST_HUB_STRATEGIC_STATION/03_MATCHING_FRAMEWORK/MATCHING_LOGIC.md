# Matching Framework: Expert Selection & Company Profiling

## Overview: The Art & Science of Pairing

Matching is not a feature. It's the **core value proposition** of TRUST HUB. While marketplaces offer search + ratings, TRUST HUB offers **intelligent selection** based on behavior, compatibility, and risk.

---

## 🔷 Matching Principles (Non-Negotiable)

### 1. **Human Judgment at Core**
- Algorithms suggest; humans decide
- No automatic matching
- Every MATCH tier project includes a human conversation

### 2. **Compatibility > Skill**
- Technical skills are table stakes
- Compatibility is the differentiator
- How someone communicates > what they've built

### 3. **Realistic Assessment**
- We say "no" to mismatched pairs
- We'd rather cancel a project than set it up for failure
- Protecting reputation is more important than commission

### 4. **Transparency Both Ways**
- Company knows what they're getting
- Expert knows what's expected
- No surprises on either side

### 5. **Long-Term Thinking**
- Preference for repeat collaborators
- Reward experts who build consistency and reputation
- Value stability over quick wins

- **Communication frequency** — Expect daily updates or weekly?
- **Process tolerance** — Rigid process or flexible?
- **Client type** — Work better with corporations or startups?

## 🧩 Matching Algorithm (Human-Centered)

### Stage 1: Automated Filter
When a company requests MATCH, system automatically flags:
- ✅ Experts with relevant skills (keyword match)
- ✅ Experts with zero disputes on similar projects
- ✅ Experts with availability in the requested timeline
- ✅ Experts whose pricing aligns with budget

**Output:** 5–10 candidate experts (technical fit)

### Stage 2: Human Review (Critical)
TRUST HUB curator reviews candidates:
- ❓ Does communication style match company's expectation?
- ❓ Has this expert worked with similar company types before?
- ❓ Does their timeline comfort match project needs?
- ❓ Any personality red flags?
- ❓ Would THIS expert, THIS company, THESE terms work together?

**Output:** 2–3 top candidates (compatibility fit)

### Stage 3: Company Conversation
During the 15-minute call, TRUST HUB learns:
- What does "success" mean to this company?
- What's their biggest fear about this project?
- How do they handle problems?
- What's non-negotiable vs. flexible?
- What communication style works for them?

**Output:** Refined understanding of company's true needs

### Stage 4: Expert Matching
TRUST HUB matches company with best expert by considering:
- **Skill match** — Can they do the work? (Yes/No)
- **Style match** — Will they click? (High/Medium/Low)
- **Risk alignment** — Are expectations realistic? (High/Medium/Low)
- **Timeline comfort** — Is it feasible? (Yes/No)
- **Budget fairness** — Is it appropriate? (Yes/No)

**Decision:** Match or re-filter and re-interview

### Stage 5: Pre-Kickoff Briefing
Before work starts, both sides get briefed by TRUST HUB:

**To Company:**
- "This is why we picked this expert"
- "Here's what they do exceptionally well"
- "Here's how they like to communicate"
- "Here's what they'll expect from you"

**To Expert:**
- "This is what success looks like to this company"
- "Here's their biggest concern"
- "Here's how they make decisions"
- "Here's what timeline they're really on"

**Result:** Both parties start with aligned expectations

- Company provides expert ID

- No matching needed (they already know each other)

|------|-----------|----------|

## 🔄 Matching Workflows

### Workflow 1: MATCH Request (Standard)

```
Company creates task + requests MATCH
              ↓
System auto-filters 5–10 candidates
              ↓
TRUST HUB curator reviews (2–3 hours)
              ↓
Top 2–3 candidates selected
              ↓
Company calls (15 min) with TRUST HUB
              ↓
Company selects preferred expert
              ↓
TRUST HUB briefs both sides
              ↓
Project starts with aligned expectations
```

**Timeline:** 2–3 days total

### Workflow 2: MANAGED (Team Assembly)

```
Company describes full project scope
              ↓
TRUST HUB analyzes requirements & breaks into roles
              ↓
Curator assembles team:
  • 1–2 experts for core work
  • 1 specialist for specific domain
  • 1 QA/review role (if needed)
              ↓
Team briefing call (all parties + TRUST HUB PM)
              ↓
Team roles, responsibilities, milestones defined
              ↓
Work begins with full transparency
```

**Timeline:** 3–5 days (faster assembly but more complex)

## ⚠️ When We Say "No" to a Match

We reject matches when:

1. **Skill gap is too large** — Expert is clearly underqualified
2. **Communication styles clash** — Company is demanding; expert is passive
3. **Timeline is unrealistic** — Expert says "possible" but we know it's risky
4. **Budget is unfair** — Company is asking for €10K work at €2K price
5. **History is problematic** — Expert has pattern of disputes
6. **Company is high-risk** — History of difficult collaborations
7. **Red flags present** — Gut feeling says "this won't work"

**Message to company:**
*"We didn't find the right fit in this case. Better to wait or restructure the project. Here's what we'd recommend instead."*

**Message to expert:**
*"This project isn't the right fit for your style. We're holding it for someone who's a better match. We'll send you projects that are."*

**This is how TRUST HUB builds credibility:** We protect both sides by saying "no" when needed.
