# Project Management & Operational Framework

## Overview: How TRUST HUB Runs Projects

Project management in TRUST HUB is **not top-heavy**. It's **transparent, fair, and focused on predictability**.

The goal is: both parties know exactly what's happening, when, and what happens next.

---

## 🔷 Core Principles of TRUST HUB Operations

### 1. **Clarity Over Complexity**
- Process must be simple enough for freelancers and SMBs to understand
- No jargon; no bureaucracy
- One-page agreements, not 50-page contracts

### 2. **Transparency at Every Step**
- Both parties see the timeline, status, and deliverables
- No hidden conversations; everything logged in platform
- Admin has access only when needed (disputes)

### 3. **Selective Human Intervention**
- Automation handles routine (status updates, notifications, fund releases)
- Humans handle decisions (scope changes, disputes, relationship building)
- TRUST HUB steps in only when it adds value

### 4. **Fair & Neutral**
- TRUST HUB protects process, not people
- Arbitration is based on contract + evidence, not personalities
- Both sides trust that TRUST HUB won't favor one over the other

### 5. **Accountability**
- Expert is accountable for deliverables
- Company is accountable for clear requirements
- TRUST HUB is accountable for fair process

## 📋 Project Phases (for All Tiers)

### Phase 0: Pre-Project (Agreement)

**For ESCRO DIRECT:**
- Company + Expert agree on deliverables (via chat or email)
- Company creates task in platform (structured form)
- Expert reviews and confirms understanding
- **Timeline:** 24–48 hours

**For ESCRO MATCH:**
- TRUST HUB does matching conversation
- Company selects expert
- TRUST HUB briefs both sides on expectations
- **Timeline:** 2–3 days

**For ESCRO MANAGED:**
- TRUST HUB has detailed project planning call
- Team assembled and briefed
- Milestone sequence defined
- **Timeline:** 3–5 days

**Deliverable from this phase:**
- Clarity document (structured, non-negotiable understanding)
- Acceptance of terms by both parties
- Funds blocked in escrow

---

### Phase 1: Kickoff (Project Start)

**What happens:**
- If MATCH/MANAGED: kickoff call with all participants
- Project timeline and milestones are confirmed in writing
- Communication norms are established (daily/weekly updates? async only?)
- First step/action items defined
- Contingency plan discussed ("What if X happens?")

**TRUST HUB role:**
- For DIRECT: No participation (expert + company handle)
- For MATCH: Optional facilitation (usually just automated notification)
- For MANAGED: Full participation + project manager assigned

**Outputs:**
- Kickoff document with agreed timeline
- Communication schedule confirmed
- First deliverable date set
- Escalation path clear ("If X problem, contact Y")

**Timeline:** 1 day before project actually starts

---

### Phase 2: Execution (Active Work)

**For ESCRO DIRECT:**
- Expert works; company watches status in platform
- Communication is direct (email, chat, platform)
- Milestones tracked informally or by expert updates
- Either party can flag issues

**For ESCRO MATCH:**
- Expert works with company oversight
- TRUST HUB available if issues arise
- Weekly or bi-weekly check-ins (if large projects)
- Early warning system: if expert says "I'm blocked," TRUST HUB tries to help resolve

**For ESCRO MANAGED:**
- TRUST HUB project manager is actively involved
- Daily standups or 3x/week check-ins (depends on project)
- Project manager removes blockers, coordinates team
- Company gets weekly status report + dashboard visibility
- Any scope changes go through TRUST HUB (prevents scope creep)

**Key practices across all tiers:**
- ✅ Written log of decisions (no "I thought you said...")
- ✅ Updates posted in platform (not lost in email)
- ✅ Issues logged with priority (blocker/high/medium/low)
- ✅ Changes tracked (original agreement vs. current state)

**When things go wrong:**
1. Early detection (expert says "I'm stuck" or company says "This isn't right")
2. Fast diagnosis (TRUST HUB asks questions within 24 hours)
3. Fair solution (Renegotiate? Refund? Partial release? Bring in another expert?)
4. Documentation (Every decision logged, both sides agree)

**Timeline:** Days to weeks (depends on project)

---

### Phase 3: Delivery & Confirmation

**Expert's responsibility:**
- Upload deliverables to platform (all artifacts)
- Write summary of what was delivered
- Mark milestone as "Ready for Review"
- Provide context: "Here's what you're looking at, here's how to use it"

**Company's responsibility:**
- Review within 72 hours (or timeline agreed)
- Either: confirm ("This is good") OR flag issues ("This isn't complete")
- If issues: specific feedback, not vague criticism
- If issues: expert gets 7 days to revise (or renegotiate timeline)

**TRUST HUB's role:**
- FOR DIRECT: None (unless dispute opens)
- FOR MATCH: Light touch (helps if there's disagreement on "done")
- FOR MANAGED: Active (validates quality, mediates disputes immediately)

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks per milestone (depending on project)

---

### Phase 4: Fund Release (The Bridge)

**When company confirms:**
- Funds for that milestone **automatically transfer** to expert's bank account
- Expert receives notification + bank transfer receipt
- Company receives final proof of payment
- Next milestone starts (if applicable)

**If company flags issues:**
- Expert has 7 days to revise (or renegotiate)
- If expert fixes: funds release automatically
- If expert can't fix: dispute opens (see Dispute Resolution section)
- If agreement on partial completion: split release (60% expert / 40% company refund, or custom)

**Key protection:**
- Funds **never** go to either party without explicit acceptance
- Expert doesn't lose money due to vague feedback
- Company doesn't lose money due to poor delivery

**Timeline:** 1–3 days (from confirmation to fund arrival in expert's account)

## ⚠️ Dispute Resolution (The Hard Part)

### When Disputes Happen:
Disputes arise when one party is unsatisfied with delivery OR when timeline/scope breaks down.

### Types of Disputes:

**Type 1: Quality Dispute**
- Company says: "This isn't what we agreed to"
- Expert says: "This is exactly what you asked for"
- **What we look at:** Original contract vs. actual delivery + communication logs

**Type 2: Completeness Dispute**
- Company says: "It's not finished"
- Expert says: "This was all you budgeted for"
- **What we look at:** Original scope vs. deliverables vs. timeline

**Type 3: Timeline Dispute**
- Company says: "You missed the deadline"
- Expert says: "You delayed approvals"
- **What we look at:** Kickoff date → actual timeline → approval lag

**Type 4: Behavior Dispute**
- Company says: "They ghosted me"
- Expert says: "They kept moving goalposts"
- **What we look at:** Communication logs, response times, scope change requests

---

### Dispute Resolution Process (72-Hour Window)

**Hour 0–24: Intake & Documentation**
- Party who's unhappy opens dispute in platform
- Provides: issue description, evidence (emails, screenshots, messages)
- Other party gets 24 hours to respond
- Both parties upload supporting docs

**Hour 24–48: Analysis by Arbitrator**
- TRUST HUB arbitrator (neutral person, not involved in project) reviews all evidence
- Asks clarifying questions to both parties (if needed)
- Checks contract against reality
- Forms preliminary assessment

**Hour 48–72: Decision & Communication**
- Arbitrator decides: Release / Partial / Refund / Escalate
- Communicates decision to both parties with reasoning
- Implements decision immediately
- Logs everything for future reference

**Post-Decision:**
- Decision is **final** for routine disputes
- Either party can escalate to TRUST HUB board for review (additional 48 hours)
- If escalated: board makes final call

---

### Dispute Outcomes (What Arbitrator Can Decide)

**Outcome 1: Full Release**
- Expert was right
- Company pays in full
- Expert gets funds
- Expert's badge improves slightly (handled dispute fairly)

**Outcome 2: Full Refund**
- Company was right
- Expert failed to deliver adequately
- Company gets refund
- Expert's badge decreases
- Expert can choose to revise (get second chance) or accept refund

**Outcome 3: Partial Split (e.g., 70/30)**
- Both sides have a point
- Expert delivered partially
- Refund covers what's incomplete
- Expert gets paid for usable work
- Most common outcome for real-world disputes

**Outcome 4: Escalate to Legal**
- Dispute is fundamental (fraud, IP violation, illegal request)
- TRUST HUB escalates to lawyers
- Funds stay in escrow until legal decides
- Rare, but option exists

---

### Dispute Prevention (The Better Path)

**TRUST HUB prioritizes prevention over resolution:**

1. **Clear kickoff** — Both parties sign off on exact deliverables
2. **Structured updates** — Regular check-ins catch misalignment early
3. **Early intervention** — If expert says "I'm blocked," we help immediately
4. **Scope gate** — Any change request goes through TRUST HUB (prevents creep)
5. **Confirmation ritual** — Company confirms they're happy BEFORE funds release

**Statistics we track:**
- % of projects with disputes (target: <5%)
- % of disputes that get escalated (target: <20% of disputes)
- % of disputes resolved to mutual satisfaction (target: >80%)

If dispute rate is high, we improve **process**, not just conflict resolution.

## 🔄 Communication Standards (Across All Tiers)

### Expected Response Times:
| Person | Situation | Expected Response |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Expert | Company question | 24 hours |
| Expert | Blocker/issue | 12 hours |
| Company | Expert question | 48 hours |
| Company | Deliverable review | 72 hours |
| TRUST HUB | Non-emergency question | 24 hours |
| TRUST HUB | Emergency/escalation | 4 hours |

### Communication Norms:
- ✅ All project communication in platform (no private channels)
- ✅ Updates posted, not scattered across email
- ✅ Decisions documented in writing
- ✅ No ambiguous language ("soon," "almost done")
- ✅ Issues flagged immediately, not hidden until deadline

### What Kills Projects:
- ❌ Ghosting (no response for 5+ days)
- ❌ Scope changes without discussion
- ❌ Vague feedback ("I don't like it")
- ❌ Hidden concerns (not saying something is wrong)
- ❌ Unilateral timeline changes

## 📊 Status Tracking (Simple Version)

### Project Status (Visibility for Company):
- 🟢 **On Track** — On schedule, no blockers
- 🟡 **At Risk** — Minor blockers, but manageable
- 🔴 **Off Track** — Major issues, likely late or over budget
- ⚫ **Blocked** — Cannot proceed without company decision/info

### Milestone Status:
- ⬜ **Not Started**
- 🔵 **In Progress** — % complete visible
- 🟡 **Review** — Delivered, awaiting company confirmation
- 🟢 **Approved** — Confirmed, funds releasing
- 🔴 **Revision** — Feedback given, expert revising

### Fund Status:
- 💰 **Held** — In escrow, not yet released
- 📤 **Processing** — Being transferred to expert
- ✅ **Released** — In expert's account
- 🔄 **Partial** — Split between expert and company

## 🎯 TRUST HUB PM Role (For MANAGED Projects Only)

### Responsibilities:
1. **Pre-project:** Work with company to define clear scope
2. **Team assembly:** Select and brief team members
3. **Kickoff:** Ensure alignment on timeline and expectations
4. **Execution:** Daily/weekly oversight; remove blockers
5. **Quality gates:** Review deliverables; ensure standard met
6. **Company liaison:** Weekly updates to company
7. **Scope control:** Review any scope changes; prevent creep
8. **Issue escalation:** Address conflicts immediately
9. **Final delivery:** Validate all milestones complete
10. **Closure:** Project report, badge updates, lessons learned

### PM's Authority:
- Can negotiate timeline extensions (within reason)
- Can suggest team changes if someone isn't delivering
- Cannot reduce scope unilaterally
- Cannot approve major changes to budget
- Can make day-to-day operational decisions

### PM's Escalation Path:
- If expert is failing: discuss with expert first, then company
- If company is unclear: clarify expectations, get written confirmation
- If conflict: mediate; if fails, arbitration process
- If severe: bring in board for decision

## Conclusion: Why This Approach Works

The key is: **TRUST HUB doesn't manage people; it manages processes.**

When process is clear, people behave better. When behavior is clear, disputes are rare. When disputes are rare, the ecosystem becomes stronger and more attractive.

This is the operational philosophy of TRUST HUB.
