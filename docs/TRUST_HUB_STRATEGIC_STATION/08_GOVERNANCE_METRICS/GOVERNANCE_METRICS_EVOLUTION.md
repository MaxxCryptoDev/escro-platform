## 🔷 Governance Structure

### Current Model (MVP/Early Stage)
**Decision-maker:** You (founder) + operational team
- You own: Strategic decisions, arbitration, culture
- Team owns: Day-to-day operations, matching, customer support
- Board role: Advisory (if you have advisors/investors)

**What works:**
- Fast decisions
- Values stay consistent
- Culture is tight
- You see every important dispute

**What breaks:**
- Can't scale past ~200 concurrent projects
- You become the bottleneck
- Arbitration can't be objective (you know too many people)

---

### Phase 2 Model (Scaling, 12–18 months)
**Structure:** 3-person Operational Board
- Founder (you) — Culture, strategy, long-term
- Operations Lead — Day-to-day, disputes, customer relationships
- Community Lead — Community building, expertise, feedback

**Decision framework:**
- **Strategic decisions** (pricing, new service tiers, major features) → Founder final call
- **Operational decisions** (which expert to match, dispute resolution) → Team consensus
- **Community decisions** (moderation, events, partnerships) → Community lead with advisory

---

### Phase 3 Model (Mature, 24+ months)
**Structure:** Trust Board + Advisory Council
- **Trust Board** (3 external + founder) → Strategic decisions, major arbitration appeals
- **Operations Team** → Day-to-day, matching, disputes
- **Advisory Council** (6–8 experts, company leaders, external advisors) → Guidance on market needs

**Why this works:**
- Neutrality in arbitration (external board, not founder)
- Diverse perspective (not just founder's vision)
- Sustainability (less dependent on one person)
- Reputation (external oversight = credibility)

## 📊 Key Metrics (What TRUST HUB Measures)

### Tier 1: Health Metrics (Mission-Critical)

#### Growth:
- **Active companies** — Month-over-month growth (target: 20–30% in Year 1)
- **Active experts** — Month-over-month growth (target: 25–35% in Year 1)
- **Total GMV** (Gross Marketplace Volume) — Total transaction value
- **Average project size** — Revenue per transaction
- **Repeat project rate** — % of second/third projects with same parties

#### Engagement:
- **Projects per active user** — How many concurrent projects?
- **Response time** — Expert to company (lower = better)
- **Communication quality** — % of projects with clear, documented discussion
- **Utilization rate** — % of expert capacity being used

#### Trust:
- **Dispute rate** — % of projects with disputes (target: <5%)
- **Resolution rate** — % of disputes resolved to satisfaction (target: >80%)
- **Badge upgrade rate** — How fast are experts progressing to Trusted/Certified?
- **Customer satisfaction** — Post-project NPS (target: 50+)

---

### Tier 2: Quality Metrics

#### Delivery:
- **On-time delivery rate** — % of projects finished on agreed timeline (target: 90%+)
- **Quality satisfaction** — % of companies "fully satisfied" with delivery (target: 85%+)
- **Refund rate** — % of projects with partial or full refund (target: <5%)
- **Scope creep incidents** — How often does scope expand mid-project? (target: <10%)

#### Behavior:
- **Expert response time** — Average hours to respond to inquiry (target: <24h)
- **Company payment reliability** — % of on-time payments (target: 98%+)
- **Chargeback rate** — % of funded projects with chargebacks (target: <1%)
- **Repeat partnership rate** — % of companies/experts who work together 2+ times (target: 40%+)

---

### Tier 3: Business Metrics

#### Revenue:
- **Total revenue** — All sources (escrow commission + subscriptions + management fees)
- **Revenue per tier** — How much comes from DIRECT vs. MATCH vs. MANAGED?
- **Subscription revenue** — Recurring monthly (predictable revenue)
- **Customer acquisition cost** — How much to get one new company?
- **Customer lifetime value** — How much does one company spend over time?

#### Efficiency:
- **Operational cost per transaction** — Cost to run one project
- **Support tickets per project** — How many issues need help?
- **Dispute resolution cost** — Time/resource per dispute
- **Margin by service tier** — Profitability per tier

---

### Tier 4: Strategic Metrics

#### Market Position:
- **Market awareness** — % of target market that knows about TRUST HUB
- **Brand perception** — How do companies/experts talk about us?
- **Competitive positioning** — How many similar services in Romania? (currently: 0)
- **Strategic partnerships** — Collaborations with chambers, industry groups

#### Community Health:
- **Expert retention** — % of experts who stay active (target: 60%+)
- **Company retention** — % of companies who return (target: 70%+)
- **Community engagement** — Posts, discussions, participation in groups
- **Badge health** — % of users at each badge level

## 🌍 Long-Term Vision: The Clearing House Thesis

### Current State (MVP):
- TRUST HUB = marketplace with escrow
- Value: Safer transactions, better matching
- Risk: Lots of competition (Upwork, Toptal, others)
- Moat: Human judgment + trust

### Future State (Clearing House):
- TRUST HUB = infrastructure for B2B transactions
- All B2B freelance/expert work flows through TRUST HUB
- Value: Standard payment processing (like banks for B2B)
- Moat: Regulatory, data, network effects

### How We Get There:

**Step 1: Prove it works (Year 1)**
- Show that TRUST HUB transactions are safer, faster, clearer
- Build enough volume to have data
- Establish trust with first mover advantage

**Step 2: Become the standard (Years 1–2)**
- Partner with industry bodies (chambers of commerce, associations)
- "Verified by TRUST HUB" becomes recognizable
- Experts compete to have badges; companies prefer TRUST HUB
- Network effects kick in

**Step 3: Build infrastructure (Years 2–3)**
- Move beyond matching to full workflow management
- Integrate tools (invoicing, tax, reporting)
- Become essential to how B2B works
- Think: Stripe for payments, TRUST HUB for expertise

**Step 4: Scale internationally (Years 2–4)**
- Replicate model in other EU countries
- Local operations, global standard
- Eventually: Global clearing house for B2B expertise transactions

### Why This Works:
1. **Regulatory advantage** — We're not just a platform; we're infrastructure
2. **Network effects** — The more companies use it, the more experts join
3. **Data moat** — We learn what works; we predict matches better
4. **Switching costs** — Once you're in TRUST HUB, your reputation is tied to it
5. **Trust moat** — Hard to replicate (takes time and culture)

## 🔄 How You Evolve Your Role

### Year 1: Builder/Founder
- You're coding, matching, arbitrating, marketing

## 📋 Annual Strategic Review (What You Evaluate)

**Every 12 months, ask:**

1. **Are we on mission?**
   - Is TRUST HUB still focused on trust, not just transactions?
   - Are both sides (companies + experts) being treated fairly?

2. **Is the business sustainable?**
   - Do we have a clear path to profitability?
   - Are unit economics getting better?
   - Can we compete long-term?

3. **Is the market right?**
   - Is B2B collaboration becoming more digital?
   - Are people willing to pay for clarity?
   - Is fraud still an issue we solve?

4. **Is the team right?**
   - Do we have the talent we need?
   - Are values still aligned?
   - Is culture strong?

5. **What's next?**
   - What new service should we build?
   - Where should we expand geographically?
   - Who should we partner with?

**If answers are good:** Keep going, scale faster
**If answers are problematic:** Pause, fix, regroup

## Conclusion: Metrics Serve Mission

Metrics aren't about vanity. They're about **knowing if we're actually building what we said we'd build**.

- **Trust metrics** tell us if we're trusted
- **Quality metrics** tell us if we're fair
- **Growth metrics** tell us if we're relevant
- **Retention metrics** tell us if people actually want us

Track these obsessively. Let them guide decisions. Ignore them at your peril.
