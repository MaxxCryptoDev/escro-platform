## ⚖️ How You Arbitrate (Concrete Examples)

### Example 1: High-Revenue Customer vs. New Expert
**Situation:** A company that spends €100K/year with TRUST HUB disputes a project from a new expert. The expert says they delivered exactly what was agreed.

**Traditional CEO answer:** Refund the company; keep the repeat customer.

**Your answer as Guarantor:** 
- Review the contract (what was agreed?)
- Check communication logs (was there clarity?)
- Review deliverables (do they match contract?)
- If the expert delivered what was promised → Expert gets paid. Company's disappointment is not a valid dispute.
- Explain the decision publicly (so people understand fairness doesn't depend on customer value)

**The cost:** You lose a high-revenue customer. You do it anyway.

---

### Example 2: Investor Pressure vs. Rule Consistency
**Situation:** Investors want to grow 50% next quarter. You see fraud patterns in new user signups. Your compliance person says you should be "more permissive" to hit growth targets.

**Traditional CEO answer:** Loosen standards; let more users in; worry about fraud later.

**Your answer as Guarantor:**
- Enforce KYC strictly
- Ban fraudulent users
- Explain to investors that growth at the cost of safety is not real growth (it's debt)
- Accept slower growth

**The cost:** You miss growth targets. You do it anyway.

---

### Example 3: Process vs. Efficiency
**Situation:** You could speed up matching by removing the 15-minute consultation call and automating it entirely. This would improve metrics and reduce operational cost.

**Traditional CEO answer:** Automate; reduce overhead; increase profit.

**Your answer as Guarantor:**
- Keep the consultation call (human judgment matters)
- Accept lower margins
- Explain that "efficient" is not "fair" (the call exists because it catches problems)

**The cost:** You operate at lower margin. You do it anyway.
