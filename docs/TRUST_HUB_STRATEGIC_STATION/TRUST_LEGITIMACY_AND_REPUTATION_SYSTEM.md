# TRUST LEGITIMACY & REPUTATION SYSTEM
## How Infrastructure Builds Credibility Through Verification

---

## Preamble

An infrastructure is only legitimate when its decisions are verifiable and its claims are measurable.

TRUST HUB does not ask you to believe. TRUST HUB asks you to verify.

This document explains how legitimacy is earned, maintained, and lost—systematically, not subjectively.

---

## 1. PARTICIPATION CRITERIA: Who Can Join This Infrastructure?

### Entry Requirements (Mandatory for All)

Everyone who participates in TRUST HUB must meet three non-negotiable requirements:

#### Requirement 1: Identity Verification (KYC)
**What this means:**
- You are who you claim to be (verified by government ID or business registration)
- Your legal name matches your identity
- Your beneficial owner (if corporate) is disclosed
- Your location is documented

**Why:** Without verifiable identity, all other protections fail. A person cannot have reputation if they can create new identities at will.

**How it's verified:**
- Companies: Official business registration + bank verification + beneficial owner disclosure
- Experts: Passport/ID + address verification + selfie verification
- Sanctions screening: Name checked against international fraud/terrorism databases

**Consequence of falsification:**
- Immediate account suspension (permanent)
- Referral to law enforcement (if serious)
- All accounts associated banned

---

#### Requirement 2: Financial Verification
**For companies:**
- Bank account is real and in your name
- Account can receive and send payments
- No chargebacks on initial setup
- No fraud flags

**For experts:**
- Bank account is real and in your name
- Account can receive payments
- No history of refund disputes

**Why:** Without proof you can actually transfer money, the escrow system cannot function.

**How it's verified:**
- Stripe integration confirms accounts are real
- Test transaction (€0.01) sent and confirmed
- Account history reviewed for fraud patterns

**Consequence of falsification:**
- Payment methods disabled
- Access to MATCH/MANAGED suspended until resolved

---

#### Requirement 3: Behavioral Commitment (Acceptance of Terms)
**What you accept by joining:**
- You understand how disputes are resolved
- You accept the arbitration process as binding
- You commit to transparent communication
- You accept that your behavior will be recorded

**Why:** Infrastructure requires trust in process, not in people. Accepting the process is the entry cost.

**How it's verified:**
- Explicit acceptance checkbox
- Review of key documents (CONSTITUTION, OPERATIONS_FRAMEWORK)
- Quiz (3 questions) confirming understanding

**Consequence of breach:**
- Behavior violation recorded on profile
- Badge downgrade or suspension
- Possible account termination

---

### Entry Restrictions (Who Cannot Join)

You are automatically rejected if:

**Fraud disqualifiers:**
- Your identity matches a known fraud/scam database
- You are sanctioned (terrorist watch list, fraud registry)
- You have been permanently banned from TRUST HUB before
- You are a duplicate account of someone already banned

**Financial disqualifiers:**
- Your bank account is flagged for money laundering
- You have 5+ chargebacks in your banking history
- You are politically exposed person without regulatory compliance
- You are a shell company with no real business activity

**Legal disqualifiers:**
- Your business is illegal (drugs, weapons, fraud)
- You are under court-ordered restraint from financial services
- You are an active defendant in active fraud cases
- You are subject to sanctions from government agencies

**Behavioral disqualifiers:**
- You have been permanently banned from another major B2B platform (recent)
- You have publicly stated intent to defraud
- You have credible evidence of organized fraud ring membership

---

- Can see the platform but cannot take action
- Restrictions: None yet (but you're being observed)

## 2. REPUTATION: What Verifiable Trust Actually Looks Like

### The Core Problem: Declared vs Validated Reputation

**Declared reputation** is what you claim about yourself:
- "I have 10 years of experience"
- "I've managed teams of 20"
- "My clients love me"
- "I've delivered $5M in projects"

**Declared reputation is worthless.** Anyone can claim anything.

**Validated reputation** is what the system can verify:
- You completed 7 projects in TRUST HUB
- All 7 were on time
- 6 were rated 4.8/5; 1 was rated 4.2/5
- Zero disputes
- Response time averaged 8 hours

**Validated reputation is everything.** It is verifiable, measurable, and consequential.

---

### Three Dimensions of Verifiable Reputation

#### Dimension 1: Completion (Do You Finish What You Start?)

**What we measure:**
- Total projects completed (count)
- On-time delivery rate (percentage)
- Project completion rate (% started that finished vs abandoned)
- Average timeline accuracy (estimated vs actual)

**Why it matters:**
- Completion shows you follow through
- Timeline accuracy shows you understand your own capacity
- Abandonment rate shows reliability under pressure

**Visible to public?** YES
- Your profile shows: "47 projects completed, 92% on time"
- Shows timeline estimates vs actual (transparency on accuracy)
- Shows completion rate (almost nobody abandons projects unless something is wrong)

**Example of strong signal:**
- 30 projects, 28 completed, 2 abandoned
- Abandonment rate: 6.7% (slightly high, but explainable)
- On-time rate: 90% (good)
- Visible interpretation: "This person finishes almost everything, mostly on time, and rarely abandons"

**Example of weak signal:**
- 50 projects, 35 completed, 15 abandoned
- Abandonment rate: 30% (very high; red flag)
- On-time rate: 45% (low)
- Visible interpretation: "This person is unreliable"

---

#### Dimension 2: Quality (Do Deliverables Meet Agreement?)

**What we measure:**
- Customer satisfaction (1–5 scale)
- Dispute rate (% of projects with disputes)
- Revision requests (how often does customer ask for rework?)
- Refund requests (how often does customer want money back?)

**Why it matters:**
- Quality shows you understand requirements
- Low dispute rate shows communication is clear
- Few revision requests show work matches expectations
- Few refunds show customer agrees you delivered value

**Visible to public?** YES
- Your profile shows: "4.6/5 average rating over 47 projects"
- Shows dispute history (if any; otherwise shows "0 disputes")
- Shows revision rate (if publicly available)

**Example of strong signal:**
- 4.7/5 rating over 30 projects
- 0 disputes
- 1 revision request across all projects
- Visible interpretation: "This person delivers what you ask for, first time"

**Example of weak signal:**
- 3.2/5 rating over 20 projects
- 4 disputes (20% dispute rate)
- 12 revision requests (60% of projects needed rework)
- Visible interpretation: "This person's work doesn't match expectations"

---

#### Dimension 3: Accountability (Do You Own Your Mistakes?)

**What we measure:**
- How you respond to criticism (defensive or learning?)
- How you handle disputes (fight or solve?)
- How you handle refund requests (refuse or negotiate?)
- Whether you offer corrections proactively

**Why it matters:**
- Accountability shows character (not just skill)
- How you handle failure is more important than never failing
- Someone who says "I made a mistake, here's how I'll fix it" is more trustworthy than someone who blames the customer

**Visible to public?** PARTIALLY
- Your profile shows dispute resolution pattern
- Shows whether you've accepted refunds or fought them
- Shows whether projects with issues were eventually resolved favorably
- Does NOT show specific disputes (those are confidential)

**Example of strong signal:**
- 1 dispute out of 25 projects (4% rate; normal)
- Dispute: Customer said deliverable wasn't right
- Resolution: You offered rework; customer accepted; both rated each other 4+
- Visible interpretation: "This person takes responsibility and fixes problems"

**Example of weak signal:**
- 6 disputes out of 25 projects (24% rate; very high)
- Pattern: You consistently refuse refunds; customers escalate to arbitration
- Most disputes resolved against you
- Visible interpretation: "This person doesn't take responsibility"

---

### Trust Score (The Aggregate Signal)

Your reputation across all three dimensions produces a **trust score (0–100)**.

**Score calculation:**
- Completion dimension: 40% of score
- Quality dimension: 35% of score
- Accountability dimension: 25% of score

**Example:**
- You have 30 projects, 28 completed (completion: 90/100)
- 4.6/5 rating, 1 dispute resolved fairly (quality: 95/100)
- Respond to feedback constructively, proactive on issues (accountability: 85/100)
- **Trust score: (90 × 0.4) + (95 × 0.35) + (85 × 0.25) = 36 + 33 + 21 = 90/100**

**What the score means:**
- 90/100 = You are highly trustworthy based on demonstrated behavior
- This is visible to companies; it influences matching and opportunity access

---

### Reputation Decay (Trust Deteriorates Over Time Without Proof)

Your reputation is not permanent. It must be maintained by continued good behavior.

**Rules of decay:**

**Inactivity decay:**
- If you haven't completed a project in 3 months, your badge is marked "inactive"
- Badge doesn't disappear, but companies see "Last active: 3 months ago"
- If inactive for 6 months, you drop one badge level (Certified → Trusted)
- If inactive for 12 months, you drop to Trial
- **Why:** Reputation requires ongoing evidence, not historical proof

**Quality decay:**
- If your average rating drops below 4.2/5 on recent projects (last 5), you receive warning
- If it drops below 4.0/5, you move to "Under Review" status
- Companies see: "This expert's recent work is below standard"
- You can recover by demonstrating improved quality
- **Why:** Past excellence doesn't excuse present mediocrity

**Behavior decay:**
- If you have 2+ disputes in a 6-month period, dispute rate is flagged
- Flagged status prevents access to MATCH/MANAGED until resolved
- **Why:** Pattern behavior (not single mistakes) indicates systemic problem

## 3. LEGITIMACY: How TRUST HUB Earns (and Loses) Authority

### What Legitimacy Means

Legitimacy is not the right to control. Legitimacy is the earned credibility that makes your decisions acceptable to both sides.

TRUST HUB has legitimacy when:
1. **It enforces rules consistently** — Same rules apply to everyone
2. **It explains decisions transparently** — People understand why, not just what
3. **It corrects its own mistakes** — When it errors, it admits and fixes
4. **It protects both sides equally** — Neither side is favored
5. **It publishes precedent** — Decisions become predictable through published patterns

Without these, TRUST HUB is not an infrastructure; it is an authority. Authorities can be challenged. Infrastructures are trusted because they are predictable.

---

### How Legitimacy Is Earned

#### Mechanism 1: Transparent Rule Application

**What this means:**
- Rules are published (everyone knows them)
- Rules apply to everyone (including TRUST HUB founders/team)
- Exceptions require board approval (not arbitrary)
- Exceptions are published (so people see the process)

**Example of legitimate decision:**
```
Policy: Experts with 3+ disputes in 6 months lose MATCH access
Applied to: Expert A (high-revenue client) — Lost access
Reason: 4 disputes in 5 months meets threshold
Transparency: Decision published; Expert A can appeal; decision reasoning visible
Result: People see: "Rules apply equally; even valuable people follow them"
```

**Example of illegitimate decision:**
```
Policy: Experts with 3+ disputes in 6 months lose MATCH access
Applied to: Expert B — Lost access (meets threshold)
Not applied to: Expert A (4 disputes, but "too valuable to suspend")
Transparency: Decision hidden; no explanation given
Result: People see: "Rules are arbitrary; big clients get special treatment"
```

#### Mechanism 2: Explainable Decisions

**What this means:**
- For every major decision, TRUST HUB publishes reasoning
- Reasoning is auditable (not vague marketing language)
- Anyone can contest the reasoning (appeals process exists)

**Example of explainable decision:**
```
Decision: Suspended user [Anonymous ID] for KYC fraud

Reasoning:
1. User submitted government ID
2. ID matched sanctions watch list (OFAC)
3. User cannot participate in TRUST HUB while on sanctions list
4. Legal requirement: TRUST HUB cannot process payments for sanctioned persons
5. Appeal process available: User can provide alternative ID or prove misidentification

Published: YES (decision framework public, identity kept confidential)
```

**Example of unexplainable decision:**
```
Decision: Removed [Expert] from platform

Reasoning: "This user is not a good fit for the community"

Published: NO

Why this is illegitimate:
- No specific rule violation named
- "Not a good fit" is subjective
- User cannot appeal; no clear path forward
- Other users wonder: "What actually happened? Could it happen to me?"
```

#### Mechanism 3: Error Correction

**What this means:**
- When TRUST HUB makes a mistake, it admits it publicly
- Correction is implemented; affected users are compensated
- Pattern is reviewed (to prevent recurrence)

**Example of legitimate error correction:**
```
Error discovered: Arbitration team applied wrong contract version to dispute

Impact: Expert lost €2,000 because arbitration judgment referenced outdated terms

Action:
1. TRUST HUB acknowledged error publicly
2. Expert refunded €2,000 plus interest (€100)
3. Original arbitration decision overturned
4. Correct arbitration completed
5. Process review conducted; changes made to prevent recurrence
6. Statement published explaining all of the above

Result: Trust increase ("They admit mistakes and fix them")
```

**Example of illegitimate non-correction:**
```
Error discovered: TRUST HUB support team deleted expert's portfolio by accident

Impact: Expert loses visibility; projects dry up

Action: Silence. No acknowledgment. No restoration. No compensation.

Result: Trust collapse ("They make mistakes but don't take responsibility")
```

---

### How Legitimacy Is Lost

TRUST HUB loses legitimacy through five specific failures:

#### Failure 1: Hidden Conflicts of Interest

**What happens:**
- TRUST HUB makes a decision that benefits TRUST HUB financially
- The conflict is not disclosed
- Decision favors TRUST HUB over participants

**Example:**
- TRUST HUB is losing money on one service tier
- Decision: Increase commission without participant approval
- Hidden: TRUST HUB's financial pressure is not disclosed
- Impact: Looks like arbitrary rule change
- Result: Legitimacy damage ("They lied about their incentives")

**How TRUST HUB prevents this:**
- Conflicts of interest are disclosed in advance
- Financial data is published (revenue, margins, costs)
- Committee reviews decisions that benefit TRUST HUB financially
- Participants can object and are heard

---

#### Failure 2: Inconsistent Rule Application

**What happens:**
- Company A violates rule X; gets suspended
- Company B violates same rule X; gets warned (no suspension)
- No explanation for difference

**Example:**
- Rule: "Chargebacks result in account review"
- Company A: 1 chargeback → Account suspended for investigation
- Company B: 2 chargebacks → Account still active; warning issued
- No explanation published
- Result: Legitimacy collapse ("Rules don't apply equally")

**How TRUST HUB prevents this:**
- Rules have published enforcement guidelines
- Severity levels are documented (what triggers what consequence)
- Deviations from guidelines require board approval
- Deviations are published with reasoning

---

#### Failure 3: Unexplained Decisions

**What happens:**
- User is banned, suspended, or restricted
- TRUST HUB doesn't explain why
- User can't appeal because they don't understand the charge

**Example:**
- Expert's account disabled
- Only message: "This account has been suspended due to violations"
- Expert doesn't know which violations
- Can't contest because reasoning is secret
- Result: Legitimacy collapse ("Arbitrary authority")

**How TRUST HUB prevents this:**
- Every account action includes specific reasoning
- Reasoning references specific policy/rule violated
- Appeal process is automatic (user does not have to request)
- Appeal is reviewed by different arbiter than original decision

---

#### Failure 4: Unequal Arbitration

**What happens:**
- Disputes are decided differently based on participant characteristics
- High-revenue customer disputes → Always favored
- New customer disputes → Always ruled against

**Example:**
- Company A (spends €50K/year): Disputes resolution = 80% favorable rate
- Company B (spends €5K/year): Disputes resolution = 30% favorable rate
- Same dispute situations, different outcomes based on customer value
- Result: Legitimacy collapse ("Arbitration is corrupt")

**How TRUST HUB prevents this:**
- Arbitration assignments are randomized (arbiter doesn't know customer value)
- Arbiters don't see customer history (only contract and dispute)
- Decisions are reviewed for bias patterns (annually, externally)
- If bias is detected, decisions are overturned and arbiters are retrained/removed

---

#### Failure 5: Governance Capture

**What happens:**
- TRUST HUB changes rules to benefit founders/insiders
- Changes are made without stakeholder input
- Participants realize they have no voice

**Example:**
- Original rule: "Commission capped at 5%"
- Founder decides: "Change commission to 10% without notice"
- Participants wake up to higher costs
- No vote, no consultation, no opt-out
- Result: Legitimacy collapse ("System captured by insider interests")

**How TRUST HUB prevents this:**
- Major rule changes require stakeholder comment period (30 days)
- Changes require board approval (not founder unilateral decision)
- Changes are published in advance (not surprise implementation)
- Participants can object; objections are considered in final decision

## 4. CONFLICT CATEGORIES: Not All Disputes Are the Same

TRUST HUB's role differs depending on conflict severity and type.

### Type 1: Transactional Conflicts (Routine)

**What this is:**
- Company and expert disagree on deliverables
- "Is this complete?" — Both have different interpretation
- Typical resolution: Clarify contract, allow revision, resolve

**Scale:** <€5,000 or straightforward

**Who decides:** Automated arbiter (trained decision-maker, not founder)

**Timeline:** 72 hours

**Example:**
- Company: "Website doesn't look like mockup"
- Expert: "I delivered exactly what you asked for"
- Arbiter: Looks at mockup vs delivery → Finds 2 CSS differences → Expert revises → Resolved
- Time: 24 hours
- Cost to TRUST HUB: €50 in arbiter time

---

### Type 2: Behavioral Conflicts (Pattern-Based)

**What this is:**
- One party shows pattern of problematic behavior
- Not a single bad deliverable, but systemic issue
- Example: Expert has 4 disputes in 6 months (all different companies)

**Scale:** Multi-project pattern

**Who decides:** Governance committee

**Timeline:** 1–2 weeks

**Example:**
- Expert has 4 disputes across 4 projects
- Pattern: Each time, expert misses deadline; customer escalates
- Analysis: Expert is consistently underestimating timeline
- Action: Expert suspended from MANAGED projects; required to take timeline training
- Timeline accuracy improves; suspension lifted
- Cost to TRUST HUB: €500 in committee review + training resources

---

### Type 3: Systemic Conflicts (Infrastructure-Level)

**What this is:**
- Decision affects multiple participants or sets precedent
- Example: Rule interpretation that affects how all contracts work
- Example: Discovered fraud pattern that impacts platform integrity

**Scale:** Company-wide

**Who decides:** Board + external legal review

**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks

**Example:**
- Discovered: Company has been using multiple accounts to evade suspension
- Impact: Affects how we detect multi-account fraud
- Decision: New fraud detection system required
- Participants affected: All users (process changes)
- Communication: Published explanation of discovery and response
- Cost to TRUST HUB: €5K in system development + legal review

---

### Type 4: Criminal Conflicts (Out of Scope)

**What this is:**
- Fraud, theft, harassment, extortion, violence
- Not a business dispute; it's a crime

**Scale:** Serious criminal conduct

**Who decides:** Law enforcement (TRUST HUB is witness/victim, not judge)

**Timeline:** Referral immediate

**Example:**
- Expert claims company stole their code and sold it
- Expert wants TRUST HUB to recover damages
- TRUST HUB response: This is IP theft (criminal); report to police; freeze both accounts pending investigation
- TRUST HUB role: Cooperate with police; preserve evidence; no arbitration
- Cost to TRUST HUB: Legal fees for cooperation with law enforcement

## 5. MEDIATION vs ARBITRATION: What TRUST HUB Actually Does

### TRUST HUB Does NOT Play Judge

TRUST HUB is not a court. It is an arbiter within a system.

**What arbitration is NOT:**
- Finding truth (courts do that)
- Distributing justice (courts do that)
- Punishing wrongdoing (courts do that)
- Rewarding good behavior (market does that)

**What arbitration IS:**
- Enforcing the agreement that both parties made
- Applying the rules consistently
- Resolving disputes using evidence from the contract + communication

---

### Three Dispute Resolution Levels

#### Level 1: Mediation (Self-Resolution)

**When it happens:** BEFORE dispute is formally opened

**What TRUST HUB does:**
- Encourages company and expert to resolve themselves
- Provides communication prompts ("Here's what the contract says...")
- Explains process ("If you can't agree, here's what happens next")
- Does NOT decide; does NOT favor either side

**Timeline:** 7 days

**Success rate:** ~40% of issues resolve at this level

**Example:**
- Company: "Deliverable doesn't match requirements"
- Expert: "I did exactly what you asked"
- TRUST HUB mediation: "Let's look at the agreement. [Points to contract]. The contract says X. Your deliverable shows Y. What's the difference?"
- Result: Company realizes expert DID deliver what was asked; company rejects their own complaint
- Resolution: Mediation success; no arbitration needed

---

#### Level 2: Arbitration (Binding Decision)

**When it happens:** If mediation fails or issue is objective

**What TRUST HUB does:**
- Reviews contract and evidence
- Makes binding decision (both sides must accept)
- Explains decision (reasoning is public)
- Enforces decision (refunds, holds, etc.)

**Who decides:** Trained arbiter (not founder, ideally not someone with relationship to either party)

**Timeline:** 72 hours (urgent) or 7 days (standard)

**Success rate:** ~95% of arbitrations resolve dispute

**Example:**
- Contract: "Deliver website with 5 pages"
- Expert delivered: 4 pages + 1 page placeholder
- Dispute: "This is not complete"
- Arbitration: "Agreement requires 5 complete pages. Delivery has 4 complete + 1 incomplete. Expert has not fulfilled agreement. Expert gets 7 days to complete page 5 or company gets 50% refund."
- Outcome: Expert completes page 5; funds released; dispute closed

---

#### Level 3: Appeal (Governance Review)

**When it happens:** If arbitration was clearly wrong or process was violated

**What TRUST HUB does:**
- Reviews arbiter's decision for process errors
- Does NOT re-arbitrate (doesn't re-examine evidence)
- Checks: Did arbiter follow rules? Did arbiter ignore evidence? Did arbiter have conflict of interest?
- Overturns decision ONLY if process was violated

**Who decides:** Board member (external to arbitration team)

**Timeline:** 14 days

**Success rate:** ~10% of appeals overturn original decision

**Example:**
- Original arbitration: Expert lost €5,000 because arbiter said delivery was late
- Appeal claim: "But the company delayed approvals; delay wasn't expert's fault"
- Appeal review: Looks at contract (didn't mention approval delays) and evidence (communication shows delay)
- Appeal decision: Original arbitration correct; contract was clear; delays don't apply
- Outcome: Appeal denied; original decision stands

## 6. LIMITS OF INFRASTRUCTURE AUTHORITY: Where TRUST HUB Stops

TRUST HUB has clear boundaries. It does NOT control:

### Limit 1: TRUST HUB Does Not Set Prices

**What this means:**
- Companies set budgets for projects
- Experts set rates for services
- TRUST HUB does not intervene in pricing

**Exception:** TRUST HUB can warn if pricing is unrealistic
- "This budget is 30% below market rate for this scope"
- "This timeline is impossible for this scope; expert has recommended 2x timeline"
- But ultimately, company and expert negotiate

**Why this limit exists:** Pricing is market-driven. Infrastructure maintains the process; market sets value.

---

### Limit 2: TRUST HUB Does Not Control Scope

**What this means:**
- Company and expert agree on what's included
- TRUST HUB does not police what they agree to
- If they agree to "everything imaginable for €1K," that's their choice (bad choice, but their choice)

**Exception:** For MANAGED projects, scope changes require approval
- Because TRUST HUB is project manager
- Scope creep is actively managed

**Why this limit exists:** Autonomy. Parties should be free to make agreements, even bad ones. TRUST HUB prevents fraud; doesn't prevent bad decisions.

---

### Limit 3: TRUST HUB Does Not Control Timeline

**What this means:**
- Company and expert set deadlines
- TRUST HUB doesn't override their agreement
- If expert says "Yes, I can do this in 1 week" and then struggles, that's their problem

**Exception:** TRUST HUB tracks timeline accuracy
- Expert who consistently misses deadlines gets flagged
- Repeated pattern affects badge

**Why this limit exists:** Capacity differs. One person's 1 week is another person's 1 month. Infrastructure doesn't dictate capacity; it measures reliability.

---

### Limit 4: TRUST HUB Does Not Define Quality

**What this means:**
- Company defines what "good" means
- Expert delivers to that definition
- TRUST HUB arbitrates if there's disagreement about whether definition was met

**TRUST HUB does NOT:**
- Say "This work is good" or "This work is bad"
- Have aesthetic judgment
- Override customer satisfaction
- Define professional standards

**TRUST HUB DOES:**
- Check: Does deliverable match the written agreement?
- Check: Did both sides understand what "done" meant?
- Enforce: The agreement that was made

**Why this limit exists:** Quality is subjective. Infrastructure enforces contracts; doesn't define beauty.

---

### Limit 5: TRUST HUB Does Not Provide Refunds Outside Arbitration

**What this means:**
- You paid for work; work was delivered; you don't like it
- TRUST HUB does not refund "just because"
- You must prove deliverable doesn't match contract

**Exception:** TRUST HUB will mediate/arbitrate disputes
- If you can show contract violation, TRUST HUB supports refund
- If you're just unhappy with an agreed deliverable, that's not TRUST HUB's problem

**Why this limit exists:** Contract finality. Once you approve, you own the deliverable. Infrastructure protects against fraud, not buyer's remorse.

---

### Limit 6: TRUST HUB Does Not Enforce Work Methods

**What this means:**
- Expert can work how they want (as long as deliverables match agreement)
- TRUST HUB doesn't care if expert works nights, weekends, or outsources
- TRUST HUB only cares: Does deliverable match contract?

**Exception:** For MANAGED projects
- TRUST HUB may require check-ins, demos, process compliance
- Because TRUST HUB PM is accountable for delivery

**Why this limit exists:** Freedom. Infrastructure defines outputs, not processes.

---

### Limit 7: TRUST HUB Does Not Engage in Outside Disputes

**What this means:**
- Company and expert work outside TRUST HUB (offline, direct payment)
- Later dispute emerges
- TRUST HUB cannot arbitrate (no contract was on platform)

**TRUST HUB's role:** None. This is an outside dispute. Use courts.

**Why this limit exists:** Infrastructure only covers what happens within it. If you circumvent TRUST HUB escrow, you lose TRUST HUB protection.

---

### Limit 8: TRUST HUB Does Not Handle Criminal Conduct

**What this means:**
- Fraud, theft, harassment, extortion, blackmail = criminal
- TRUST HUB refers to police
- TRUST HUB does not arrest, prosecute, or punish

**TRUST HUB's role:** Freeze accounts, preserve evidence, cooperate with law enforcement

**Why this limit exists:** Infrastructure maintains business process. Crimes are for courts/police.

## 7. ABUSE PREVENTION: How TRUST HUB Prevents Power Imbalances

### The Core Problem

Infrastructure is only legitimate if it prevents the powerful from dominating the weak.

Without abuse prevention, "fairness" just means "fair to people with power."

---

### Prevention Mechanism 1: Anonymized Arbitration

**What this means:**
- Arbiters do not know customer value, customer history, or participant status
- Arbiter sees only:
  - The contract (what was promised)
  - The evidence (what happened)
  - The dispute (what's being contested)

**Arbiter does NOT see:**
- Customer's spending history (€500K or €100?)
- Expert's badge level (Certified or Trial?)
- Either party's size/power (startup or Fortune 500?)
- Previous disputes (first-time or pattern?)

**Why this prevents abuse:**
- Arbiter cannot favor high-value customers
- Expert cannot be intimidated by company size
- Small company is treated same as large company
- New expert is treated same as Certified expert

**Example:**
- Company A spends €100K/year with TRUST HUB
- Expert is Trial badge (brand new)
- Dispute emerges (deliverable quality disagreement)
- Arbiter knows: "Dispute is about X"
- Arbiter does NOT know: "Company is huge; expert is tiny"
- Result: Arbiter treats them equally

---

### Prevention Mechanism 2: Randomized Arbiter Assignment

**What this means:**
- Disputes are assigned to arbiters randomly
- Parties cannot request specific arbiters
- Founder cannot assign disputes to "friendly" arbiters

**Why this prevents abuse:**
- Cannot build relationships with arbiters who favor your side
- Corruption becomes impossible (you don't know who will decide)
- Pattern of favoritism becomes visible (if one arbiter always rules for companies)

---

### Prevention Mechanism 3: Conflict of Interest Automatic Recusal

**What this means:**
- If arbiter has worked with either party before, arbiter is automatically replaced
- If arbiter has personal relationship with either party, arbiter recuses
- If arbiter's decision benefits them financially, they recuse

**Why this prevents abuse:**
- Friendship cannot influence arbitration
- Financial incentives are removed
- Personal bias is structural eliminated

---

### Prevention Mechanism 4: Audit Trail (All Decisions Published)

**What this means:**
